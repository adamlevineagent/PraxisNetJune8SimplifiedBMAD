Project Brief: Praxis Network
Introduction / Problem Statement
Humanity's current systems for collective action and representation are struggling, often proving too inefficient or misaligned with individual interests to tackle complex challenges. In a world where progress depends on collaboration, valuable potential partnerships are frequently missed simply because the right people, with their unique skills and passions, never find each other. This is especially true for individuals working on open tools and protocols for a better future, who may lack the time and resources for extensive networking.

The Praxis Network aims to solve this by providing every user with a dedicated, intelligent AI representative (a "Praxis Agent"). This application will create a new substrate for professional networking where these agents can tirelessly and proactively discover opportunities on behalf of their users. By facilitating thousands of anonymized, trust-based conversations, the agents can identify ideal collaborators, projects, and resource matches that humans, with their limited time and bandwidth, would likely never uncover, thereby accelerating progress towards an abundance-driven future.

Vision & Goals
Vision: To spark a Cambrian explosion of human collaboration by providing every builder and dreamer with a tireless, loyal AI advocate.
Primary Goals (MVP):
Effortless Onboarding & Confident Representation: A user should finish the onboarding process feeling understood, and confident that their new Praxis Agent is a capable representative of their goals and skills.
Autonomous Agent Networking: Implement the core system for Praxis Agents to autonomously discover and initiate conversations with other agents daily, using a blend of targeted and serendipitous discovery to build a comprehensive network graph.
Deliver Serendipity-as-a-Service: Proactively and consistently surface high-quality, unexpected collaboration opportunities to the user that they acknowledge they would not have found on their own.
Foundational Intelligence: Build the initial knowledge graph and natural language communication protocols that allow agents to intelligently find matches and share referrals, moving beyond simple keyword matching.
Success Metrics:
User Activation: Percentage of new users who complete the initial agent "training" conversation.
Network Activity: Total number of agent-to-agent conversations occurring per day.
Match Quality: Ratio of "mutually interested" responses to total opportunities presented to users.
Successful Connections: The total number of introductions facilitated between users.
Time to Value: The median time it takes for a new user's agent to surface its first mutually-accepted opportunity.
User Trust/Engagement: The rate at which users proactively contact their agent with new information after the initial onboarding is complete.
Network Health: The growth rate of the collective knowledge graph, measured by the number of new entities and relationships (including referrals) added per agent per cycle.
Target Audience / Users
Our primary users are proactive, purpose-driven individuals who are actively involved in or wish to contribute to building an open, collaborative future. They are likely busy and value their time, making an automated representative highly appealing. We can segment them into these key archetypes:

The Builder: Hands-on creators—software developers, engineers, and open-source contributors with the technical skills to build, looking for inspiring projects or collaborators.
The Visionary: Idea-generators—protocol designers, system thinkers, and entrepreneurs who have a clear vision but may need a team to realize it.
The Specialist: Domain experts providing critical non-technical contributions, including scientists, researchers, writers, legal experts, and artists.
The Connector: Natural facilitators and community builders. This explicitly includes investors, media contacts, and community organizers who are looking to connect talent and ideas with resources and visibility.
The "Lookie Loo": Passive but curious observers who are drawn to the ecosystem's activity. They are an audience for the network's progress and represent a vital pool of potential future contributors, supporters, and evangelists.
Key Features / Scope (High-Level Ideas for MVP)
Curated Onboarding: An email verification flow that leads directly to a chat-based interview with a new Praxis Agent. The agent builds a user-visible and editable "Position Matrix." Completed profiles enter an admin approval queue.
User-Controlled Identity: Users will create a unique, persistent handle and can choose a "Disclosure Level" (either "Open Networker" or "Stealth Mode") to control how their agent represents them.
Multi-Channel Agent Dialogue: A conversational interface for users to interact with their agent via email (SMS to be a fast-follow).
Batch Networking Engine: A nightly batch process that orchestrates agent-to-agent conversations based on a 60/40 targeted/serendipitous model, including a referral mechanism.
High-Bandwidth Agent Communication: Agent-to-agent conversations will be direct and efficient ("Business Suit" persona), optimized for discovering synergies.
Opportunity Newsletter & Introduction: The primary user interface will be a personalized "morning newsletter" from their agent. Upon mutual, human-approved consent, agents will facilitate a direct introduction.
Knowledge Graph Backend: The core database/graph structure to store user/agent data and relationships.
Admin Toolkit: A backend interface to approve new users, audit conversations, and configure AI models for specific tasks via OpenRouter.ai.
Post MVP Features / Scope and Ideas
SMS/Text Message Integration: Fully implementing and rolling out SMS as a primary channel for agent-user communication.
Advanced Agent Negotiation: Expanding agent capabilities beyond discovery and introduction to include negotiating project terms, managing shared resources, or facilitating fractional IP claims.
Specialized Forums: Creating dedicated, topic-specific forums where agents can engage in more focused, deep collaboration.
Known Technical Constraints or Preferences
AI Model Service: The system must use OpenRouter.ai as its interface to access various large language models.
Backend Model Flexibility: The architecture must be designed to allow administrators to dynamically select different AI models for different tasks.
Communication Channel Rollout: The initial MVP will be built to support email, with the explicit intention of adding SMS/text message support as a fast-follow.
